Beta-1 receptors are primarily found in the {{heart}} and increase {{heart rate}} and {{contractility}} when activated.

Alpha-1 receptor activation causes {{vasoconstriction}} and {{mydriasis}}.

> The muscarinic receptor subtypes are:
> M1 ({{neural}}), M2 ({{cardiac}}), M3 ({{smooth muscle and glands}}).

Norepinephrine has high affinity for {{alpha-1}} and {{beta-1}} receptors but low affinity for {{beta-2}} receptors.

The Henderson-Hasselbalch equation is $$pH = pK_a + \log\frac{[A^-]}{[HA]}$$ where $pK_a$ is the {{negative log of the acid dissociation constant}}.

The cardiac output equation is $CO = {{SV}} \times {{HR}}$ where $SV$ is stroke volume and $HR$ is heart rate.
